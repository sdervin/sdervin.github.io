CLASSES

  • Design Academy

    In Design Academy, students follow Stanford's D. School Model of Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test to create projects. Each quarter starts with a project proposal, and they spend the remainder of the quarter producing their project. Creativity and innovation are emphasized, along with self-directed inquiry and learning. Design II is available for students who complete Design I and wish to expand their use of design thinking.

  • AP Computer Science Principles

    In this class, students are taught the principles of computation, including programming, abstractions, algorithms, large data sets, internet, cyber security concerns, and computing impacts. They learn to use to use technology to solve real-world issues. This class is available for students in grades 10-12, and it aims to broaden computer science participation.

  • AP Computer Science A

    This class focuses on Java, problem solving, design strategies, data structures, algorithms, solution analytics, and ethical consequences of computing. The course is open to students in grades 11-12 starting in the fall of 2019.

  • Intro to Programming

    This course is directed at students with no prior programming experience, and teaches them the basics of HTML, CSS, programing, data, and physical computing. Students will then build websites, apps, and games using skills acquired in this course.

  • Advanced Robotics

    In this course, students will build Vex Robots and program them with Easy C for competitions.

  • The DREAM Lab is also utilized on occasion by other classes such as Digital Photography and Imaging I and II, Interior Design, Technical Theater, and Biomedical Physiology Honors.
